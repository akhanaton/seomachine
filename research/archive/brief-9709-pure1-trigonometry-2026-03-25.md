## 3. Student Pain Points (From Forums & Examiner Reports)

### What Students Actually Struggle With

1. **Solving trigonometric equations with multiple solutions** — The #1 cited difficulty. Students find one solution and stop, not understanding how to use symmetry, CAST diagrams, or periodicity to find all solutions in the given domain. Examiner reports flag this every year.

2. **Losing solutions by dividing by trig functions** — Students divide both sides of an equation by sinθ or cosθ, losing the solutions where that function equals zero. Example: sin θ cos θ = sin θ → dividing by sin θ loses the sin θ = 0 solutions. Examiners explicitly flag this.

3. **Proving identities** — Students don't know the approach: which side to start with, when to convert tan to sin/cos, when to apply sin²θ + cos²θ = 1. They attempt to "work from both sides" and arrive in the middle, which is not a valid proof structure.

4. **Exact values confusion** — Students can't recall sin 60° = √3/2 vs. cos 60° = 1/2 under exam pressure. They mix up which angle gives which value. The 30-60-90 and 45-45-90 triangles aren't internalised.

5. **Radians vs. degrees** — Calculator in wrong mode is the most common error. Students also confuse radian values (π/6, π/4, π/3) with degree equivalents. Some don't understand that the question specifies the domain in radians but they solve in degrees.

6. **Graph transformations** — Students can describe individual transformations (stretch, shift) but can't combine them. "Sketch y = 3cos(2x - π/4)" requires applying multiple transformations in the correct order.

7. **Using incorrect identities** — Examiner reports cite students using "sinθ = 1 - cosθ" (wrong!) instead of "sin²θ + cos²θ = 1". Incorrect recall under pressure.

8. **Not showing sufficient working** — Using a calculator to solve trig equations and only writing the answer is insufficient. Examiners require method marks showing the solving process.

9. **Domain errors** — When asked for solutions in 0 ≤ θ ≤ 2π, students either miss solutions or include solutions outside the domain. Related to the "multiple solutions" problem.

10. **Quadratic trig equations** — Students don't recognise that 2sin²θ - sinθ - 1 = 0 is a quadratic in sinθ. They try to apply trig identities instead of factorising.

## 4. 9709 Syllabus Scope: What's In and What's NOT

### Topic 1.5 — Trigonometry (Pure Mathematics 1)

**In scope for this article:**
- Sketch and use graphs of sine, cosine, and tangent functions (for angles of any size, in degrees or radians)
- Use exact values of sine, cosine, and tangent for 0°, 30°, 45°, 60°, 90° (and radian equivalents)
- Use the notations sin⁻¹x, cos⁻¹x, tan⁻¹x for the principal values of inverse trig functions
- Use the relationships sin²θ + cos²θ = 1 and tanθ = sinθ/cosθ
- Solve simple trigonometric equations involving the six forms (sinθ = k, cosθ = k, tanθ = k and squared variations) for a given interval
- Including use of exact values and symmetry of graphs

**NOT in scope (Pure 3 / Paper 3):**
- sec, cosec, cot (reciprocal functions)
- Compound angle formulae: sin(A±B), cos(A±B), tan(A±B)
- Double angle formulae: sin 2A, cos 2A, tan 2A
- R-formulae: asinθ + bcosθ = Rsin(θ + α)
- Factor formulae
- Integration/differentiation of trig functions

**Important overlap with Topic 1.4 (Circular Measure):**
- Radians are introduced in Circular Measure (1.4), but students need fluency with radians for trig equations
- This article should assume radian familiarity but include a "check your calculator mode" reminder
