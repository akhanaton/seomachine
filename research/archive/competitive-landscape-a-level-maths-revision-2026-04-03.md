**Dominant format: Hub pages / resource directories.** 8 of 10 results for the primary query are thin hub pages that serve as navigation to deeper resources. They contain minimal educational content themselves -- typically 200-900 words of navigation links and topic lists.

**No long-form guides dominate.** The SERP rewards breadth (covering many topics) over depth (teaching any single topic). The format that wins is "topic directory with links to sub-pages."

**Resource lists rank well** -- Resourceaholic's curated blog post from 2016 still ranks, suggesting Google values link-rich compilations.

**Product/e-commerce pages** (Collins, Amazon/CGP) occupy 2-3 positions, showing commercial intent mixed with informational.

## Strategic Opportunity Summary

### The Gap

1. **"a level maths revision" (9,900/mo)** is dominated by thin UK-board hub pages. Not a single result provides Cambridge 9709-specific revision content for this high-volume query.

2. **No content exists** that targets international Cambridge students by geography, exam session (November), or the specific challenges they face.

3. **The format that wins** is hub/directory pages, but the quality bar is extremely low -- most are 200-500 word navigation pages. A comprehensive guide combining hub structure with actual educational depth would be differentiated.

4. **Cambridge-specific long-tail queries** ("cambridge 9709 revision UAE", "a level maths prep Pakistan", "cambridge international maths revision") return mostly official pages, PDF repositories, and e-commerce listings. Zero original educational content ranks.

5. **Community needs are unmet** -- international students are asking on Quora and forums for Cambridge-aligned resources, indicating active demand that no content creator is satisfying.
