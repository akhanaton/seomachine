## 3. Student Pain Points (From Forums & Examiner Reports)

### What Students Actually Struggle With

1. **Chain rule confusion** — The #1 cited difficulty. Students can't identify the "inner" and "outer" functions, especially with nested functions. The Student Room has dozens of threads asking for chain rule help.

2. **"When do I use which rule?"** — Students confuse when to use power rule, chain rule, product rule, and quotient rule. For 9709 Pure 1, only power rule and chain rule are needed, but students don't always know this.

3. **Connected rates of change** — Students fail to recognise when the chain rule is needed for rates of change problems. They attempt solutions without the connecting derivative (e.g., dy/dt = dy/dx × dx/dt).

4. **Optimisation word problems** — Setting up the expression to differentiate from a word problem is the hardest part. Students can differentiate once they have the expression, but the translation from words to algebra trips them up.

5. **"Show that" questions** — Students don't know how much working to show or how to structure a proof-style answer. Examiner reports flag incomplete working as a persistent problem.

6. **Stationary points classification** — Confusion between first derivative test (sign change) and second derivative test (positive/negative), especially when the second derivative = 0 at the stationary point.

7. **Notation confusion** — dy/dx vs. f'(x) vs. d/dx(). Students use them interchangeably and incorrectly. Some don't understand that dy/dx is a ratio-like symbol, not a fraction.

8. **Sign errors with negative powers** — Examiner reports for 9709 (2023, 2024) consistently flag sign errors when differentiating negative or fractional powers. Students forget to convert roots and fractions to power notation first.

9. **Tangent vs. Normal** — Students mix up when to use the gradient directly (tangent) vs. the negative reciprocal (normal). Simple error, costly in marks.

10. **Not finding the y-coordinate** — When asked to find the equation of a tangent at a point, students find the gradient but forget to substitute back to get the y-value.
