## 3. Student Pain Points (Series)

### What Students Actually Struggle With

1. **Which binomial formula to use** (Very High) -- Pascal's triangle vs the general formula. Students don't know when to use which. For 9709 Pure 1, only finite positive integer n is needed (Pascal's triangle method), but students confuse this with the infinite expansion from Pure 3.

2. **Coefficient handling in binomial expansion** (High) -- When expanding (2 + 3x)^5, students forget to raise BOTH the 2 and the 3x to the appropriate powers. They write nCr * (3x)^r instead of nCr * 2^(n-r) * (3x)^r.

3. **Confusing AP and GP formulae** (High) -- Six formulae across AP and GP (nth term + sum for each, plus sum to infinity for GP). Students mix them up under exam pressure.

4. **Sum to infinity convergence condition** (High) -- Forgetting to verify |r| < 1 before applying S_infinity = a/(1-r). Examiners note students who use the formula without stating or checking the condition.

5. **Setting up simultaneous equations from word problems** (Medium) -- "The 3rd term of a GP is 12 and the 6th term is 96. Find the first term and common ratio." Students can't translate conditions into equations.

6. **Sign errors in binomial coefficients** (Medium) -- Expanding (1 - 2x)^4 requires tracking negative signs through each term. Students lose the alternating signs.

7. **"Find the term independent of x"** questions (Medium) -- Binomial expansion with mixed powers (e.g., (x + 1/x)^6). Students don't know how to set up the condition for the x^0 term.

## 3. Student Pain Points (Circular Measure)

1. **"Why do radians even exist?"** (Very High) -- Students don't understand the purpose. The answer: arc length and sector area formulae (s = rθ, A = ½r²θ) only work in radians. That's the entire motivation.

2. **Mixing degrees and radians in calculations** (High) -- Calculator set to wrong mode, or using degrees in a formula that requires radians. Examiner reports flag this consistently.

3. **Confusing sector, segment, and triangle areas** (High) -- Area of segment = area of sector - area of triangle. Students either forget to subtract or subtract the wrong thing.

4. **The perimeter trap** (High) -- Perimeter of a segment = arc length + chord length. Students give arc length only, forgetting the chord. The chord requires either cosine rule or 2r sin(θ/2).

5. **Converting between degrees and radians** (Medium) -- The conversion factor π/180 is straightforward but students make errors, especially with angles not in the standard table (30°, 45°, 60°, 90°).

6. **Not drawing diagrams** (Medium) -- Circular measure problems are highly geometric. Students who attempt them purely algebraically miss obvious relationships.
