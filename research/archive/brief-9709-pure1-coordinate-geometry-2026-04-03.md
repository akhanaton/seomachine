## 3. Student Pain Points (From Forums & Examiner Reports)

### What Students Actually Struggle With

1. **Circle equation manipulation** (Very High) -- Converting between general form (x^2 + y^2 + 2gx + 2fy + c = 0) and centre-radius form by completing the square. Sign errors are the #1 issue: students write the centre as (g, f) instead of (-g, -f).

2. **Finding tangent equations** (Very High) -- Students forget that a tangent to a circle is perpendicular to the radius at the point of contact. They find the gradient of the radius but forget to take the negative reciprocal.

3. **"When do I use which line equation form?"** (High) -- Confusion between y = mx + c, y - y1 = m(x - x1), and ax + by + c = 0. Students don't know when to use which.

4. **Perpendicular gradient relationship** (High) -- Forgetting m1 x m2 = -1. Confusing when to use perpendicular vs parallel condition.

5. **Intersection of lines and circles using discriminant** (High) -- Setting up the simultaneous equations correctly, then applying the right discriminant condition (= 0 for tangent, > 0 for two intersections, < 0 for no intersection).

6. **Not drawing diagrams** (Medium) -- Students attempt problems purely algebraically and miss geometric insights that make the problem simpler.

7. **Premature rounding with surds** (Medium) -- Square-rooting distances too early and accumulating rounding errors. Examiners flag this in "leave your answer in exact form" questions.

8. **Perpendicular bisector multi-step problems** (Medium) -- These require midpoint + gradient + negative reciprocal + line equation -- four steps where any error cascades.

9. **"Show that the line is a tangent"** (Medium) -- Students don't know whether to use the discriminant method (substitute and check b^2 - 4ac = 0) or the perpendicular distance method.

10. **Area calculations from coordinate geometry** (Medium) -- Finding the area of triangles formed by tangent lines and coordinate axes. Students don't connect the algebra to the geometric shape.
