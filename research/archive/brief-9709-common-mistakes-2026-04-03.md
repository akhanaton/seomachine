## 4. Student Pain Points (From Forums)

### The Student Room -- Recurring Themes

1. **"I know the method but still lose marks"** -- the most emotionally resonant complaint. Students feel the issue is "carelessness" but examiners see systematic errors.

2. **"Silly mistakes vs knowledge gaps"** -- students can't distinguish between random slips and recurring patterns. A mistake log/tracker is the recommended fix (multiple threads).

3. **Time pressure** -- spending too long on early questions and rushing later ones, where more marks are available.

4. **Calculator dependence** -- over-reliance on calculators for steps that need to be shown. "I got the right answer but zero marks."

5. **Not reading the question fully** -- answering a different question than the one asked. "Give your answer in exact form" means no decimals.

### Student-Recommended Strategies (from forums)
- Keep a personal error log tracking recurring mistakes
- After each practice paper, categorise errors: knowledge gap vs sign error vs reading error
- Force yourself to write every step for one full paper (builds the habit)
- Check answers by substitution or reverse operation

- The examiner observation: "Candidates who showed no working could not gain method marks"
- Why it happens: calculator dependence, time pressure
- The fix: every algebraic step gets its own line. Every integral shows the substitution. Every derivative shows the rule applied.
- Correct vs incorrect worked example (side-by-side)

H3: Mistake 2 -- Only One Trig Solution (Trigonometry)
- The examiner observation: "Many candidates found only one solution in the given interval"
- Why it happens: students use inverse trig to find one angle and stop
- The fix: always ask "how many solutions?" Sketch the graph or use CAST diagram.
- Example: sin θ = 0.5 in [0°, 360°] has TWO solutions (30° and 150°)
- Cross-link to trigonometry spoke

H3: Mistake 3 -- Sign Errors in Differentiation (Differentiation)
- The examiner observation: "Sign errors when differentiating negative powers were widespread"
- Why it happens: not converting to power notation first (√x → x^(1/2), 1/x → x^(-1))
- The fix: always rewrite roots and fractions as powers before differentiating
- Correct vs incorrect worked example
- Cross-link to differentiation spoke

H3: Mistake 4 -- Forgetting +C (Integration)
- The examiner observation: "The constant of integration was frequently omitted"
- Why it happens: it feels redundant when focused on the integration technique
- The fix: write +C immediately after integrating, before doing anything else
- Cross-link to integration spoke

H3: Mistake 5 -- Discriminant Condition Confusion (Quadratics / Coordinate Geometry)
- The examiner observation: "Some candidates used the wrong inequality for the discriminant"
- Why it happens: three conditions look similar (> 0, = 0, < 0) and students mix up which means what
- The fix: memorise all three as a set. Tangent = equal roots = b²-4ac = 0.
- Cross-link to quadratics and coordinate geometry spokes

H3: Mistake 6 -- Chain Rule Multiplier Omitted (Differentiation)
- The examiner observation: "The derivative of the inner function was often omitted"
- When differentiating (2x + 3)^4, students write 4(2x + 3)^3 instead of 4(2x + 3)^3 × 2
- Cross-link to differentiation spoke

H3: Mistake 7 -- Completing the Square Sign Errors (Quadratics / Circles)
- Affects both pure quadratics and circle equation conversion
- Cross-link to quadratics and coordinate geometry spokes

H3: Mistake 8 -- Premature Rounding (All Questions)
- "Leave your answer in exact form" means surds, fractions, or terms of π -- never decimals
- Carry at least 4 significant figures through all working

H3: Mistake 9 -- Perimeter Trap in Circular Measure (Circular Measure)
- Perimeter of segment = arc + chord, not just arc
- Cross-link to circular measure spoke

H3: Mistake 10 -- Negative Area Not Handled (Integration)
- When a curve crosses the x-axis, the definite integral gives a negative value for the region below. Students must split the integral and take absolute values.
- Cross-link to integration spoke
