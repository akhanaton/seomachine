---

## Critical Issues (Fix Immediately)

### 1. ZERO Risk Reversal (Score: 0/100)
The page asks visitors to join a waitlist but offers **no risk reduction anywhere**:
- No "free trial" mention
- No "no credit card required"
- No "cancel anytime"
- No guarantee of any kind
- No risk reversal text near any CTA button

**Impact**: This is the single biggest conversion killer. Students (and parents paying) need to know there's no financial commitment. Every competitor (Save My Exams, PMT) either has free tiers or explicit free trial language.

**Fix**: Add "Free to start. No credit card required." directly below every primary CTA. Add "Cancel anytime" near the final CTA.

### 2. CTAs Don't Align With Conversion Goal (Goal Alignment: 0/100)
The CTAs say "Join Waitlist" and "Join 500+ Students Getting Ahead" — but the page talks about features you can use NOW (diagnostic assessment, predicted grade, practice questions). This creates cognitive dissonance:
- Page promises: "Get your Exam Readiness Index in 15 minutes"
- CTA says: "Join Waitlist" (implies you can't get it yet)

**Impact**: Visitors who are sold on the product hit a wall — "wait, I can't use this now?" Either the CTAs or the page copy is misaligned.

**Fix**: If the product IS live → change CTAs to "Start Your Free Assessment" / "Get My Predicted Grade Free". If it's truly waitlist-only → rewrite page copy to be explicitly pre-launch ("Be first in line when we launch" / "Get early access").

### Value Proposition
The subheadline "Stop guessing your grade. ExamPilot's AI tells you your predicted score and creates a personalized study plan..." is actually strong copy — but the analyzers flag it because the value prop pattern isn't explicit enough. Consider reformatting:

**Current**: Paragraph below headline

### Recommendations
1. Shorten headline to under 70 characters
2. Add an explicit benefit word to headline
3. Bold the value proposition as a standalone line
4. Add "Free to start" text near above-fold CTAs
