# ExamPilot Competitor Analysis

## Primary Competitors

### 1. Save My Exams (savemyexams.com)
- **What they offer**: Past papers, revision notes, mark schemes, topic questions for GCSE and A-Level
- **Strengths**: Massive content library, broad exam board coverage, strong SEO presence, well-known brand among UK students
- **Weaknesses**: Static content (no AI, no adaptation), no predicted grades, no retention system, subscription-based for full access
- **Content strategy**: High-volume topic pages, past paper resources, revision notes. Heavy SEO — ranks for most A-Level maths queries.
- **Opportunity**: They're a static PDF repository. ExamPilot offers adaptive, intelligent practice — fundamentally different value proposition.

### 2. Physics & Maths Tutor (physicsandmathstutor.com)
- **What they offer**: Free revision notes, past papers, worksheets, topic questions
- **Strengths**: Completely free, massive organic traffic, most-cited A-Level resource by LLMs (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini)
- **Weaknesses**: Dated UI, no AI features, no progress tracking, ad-heavy, no Cambridge international focus
- **Content strategy**: SEO-optimized topic pages, free resources drive traffic, ad monetization
- **Opportunity**: PMT is the incumbent but hasn't innovated. Students use it by default because alternatives don't exist — until now.

### 3. PapaCambridge (papacambridge.com)
- **What they offer**: Cambridge International past papers, mark schemes, examiner reports
- **Strengths**: Comprehensive Cambridge archive, strong with international students
- **Weaknesses**: Pure archive site (no learning features), poor UX, no AI or adaptive features
- **Opportunity**: PapaCambridge serves Cambridge students but only with static resources. ExamPilot offers the same exam board focus with actual learning intelligence.

### 4. Revision World / StudyWise / Other Aggregators
- **What they offer**: Revision notes, flashcards, basic quizzes
- **Strengths**: Free, simple, established
- **Weaknesses**: Generic, no exam board specificity, no AI, no analytics
- **Opportunity**: These are "good enough" tools that students settle for. ExamPilot is the "actually effective" alternative.

## Indirect Competitors

### Khan Academy
- **Strengths**: World-class explanations, free, massive brand trust
- **Weaknesses**: US curriculum focus, not aligned to Cambridge/Edexcel, no exam-specific features
- **Our position**: "Khan Academy teaches you maths. ExamPilot gets you ready for YOUR exam."

### Brilliant
- **Strengths**: Interactive learning, problem-solving approach
- **Weaknesses**: Not exam-focused, not A-Level specific, subscription pricing
- **Our position**: "Brilliant builds mathematical thinking. ExamPilot builds exam readiness."

## Content Gaps We Can Exploit

1. **Predicted grades**: Nobody offers AI-predicted grades for A-Level Maths
2. **Cambridge 9709 specific content**: Massively underserved by UK-centric tools
3. **International student focus**: EU, UAE, Pakistan, Malaysia, Singapore, Nigeria — underserved by all competitors
4. **AI tutoring**: No competitor offers Socratic AI tutoring in-session
5. **Spaced repetition for A-Level Maths**: Nobody combines SRS with exam-specific content
6. **Comparison content**: "ExamPilot vs Save My Exams" — high-intent, low-competition keywords
